      Promote your business effectively

      From the course: Managing Your Small Business

      • Course details

        Getting a small business off the ground is a feat in itself. But once you've made your business idea a reality, you're faced with an array of new challenges which—if managed improperly—can dominate your time and prevent you from achieving greater success. In this course, learn strategies and techniques for running a small to medium business with confidence. Join Naomi Simson—the founder of RedBalloon and a Shark Tank Australia judge—as she shares key lessons that can help you define your purpose and values, establish a framework for success, and promote your business in a smart, cost-effective manner.

        Instructor

        • Naomi Simson

          Entrepreneur | Founder RedBalloon | Co-Founder Big Red Group (BRG)

          Naomi Simson is a businessperson, blogger, entrepreneur, author, and Shark Tank judge.

          Naomi started her entrepreneurial journey with RedBalloon in 2001 with just $25,000 and a second-hand computer. Redballoon.com.au is now considered one of Australia's online business success stories, and has proudly served more than 3.6 million customers. In past years, she's worked for big brands—including IBM, Apple, and KPMG—all of which influenced who she is today. Naomi—a LinkedIn Influencer with more than 2 million followers —is passionate about sharing her insights about business, innovation, and success with others. As a judge on Network Ten's Shark Tank, she is a keen investor and supporter of the startup community. She is also the author of the bestsellers Live What You Love and Ready to Soar.

        - Do you remember the last time you had incredible customer service? When you felt so wonderful about an experience you had you couldn't help but tell your family and friends. But equally you remember when something was really bad and you told everybody, and put it all over social media. You see brands to us, are like a person. We want to have some level of relationship with them. And if you think about this in the context of how you promote your business, ultimately great promotion reminds people that they know you, and ultimately builds some level of feeling of essence of relationship. Promotion is about building your reputation but the greatest promotion you can ever do is when other people talk about you. What about being an observer of your own business? The customer experience. "Good enough is not good enough." It has to be exemplary, amazing, something worth talking about. Would you tell stories about your own business over dinner, at the barbecue? That's what you want to happen. And with social media, that can be amplified tenfold. Your brand is held in the heart of your customers. How they think about you, how they feel about you is in fact your brand essence. The Apple meetups are exactly that. They believe they own the business and they have a right to say the destiny of the business. They want to contribute. So not only can your customers be your greatest advocate and source of promoting your business, but they can also be your greatest insight to innovations. So customers are the source of your promotion, so it's important to have a dialogue with your customers. Have open communication and listening posts, making sure you're walking in their shoes. Do you experience your business the way that they experience? Often we get so caught up 'cause we know our businesses so well. What would it be like if a complete stranger walked into your store or approached you, would they have the same customer experience? Is it consistent? Your customers are the source of your promotion. Probably if you look at your budget promotion is a big part of where you spend your money. I had absolutely not a cent when I started my business 'cause I spent the whole money on the building of the website, and I had no money left. The only way I could grow my business was if people talked about it. My job was consistently execute and listen. Do, analyze, do, listen, listen, listen. Sometimes, especially if you've been in business for a while, you kind of mix up your messages and move it around. If you want your customers to promote on your behalf you need to make sure you're very single-minded. What is it that you want to be remembered for? What is the critical thing that you want them to share and help them. Some simple, simple message. Don't expect too much but it is up to you to give them consistently a message. I rely on old fashioned marketing knowhow and it's not that hard. I need to know who my customer is, where they hang out, how many of them are there, what is it going to cost to acquire them? The other thing is I'm not alone in wanting to speak to those people. They're not unique to my business. So what if we thought who else is already talking to those customers? Maybe I could walk with them. Maybe a customer is there to be shared. Way back in the early days we got four other Ecommerce businesses together and we'd all share resources, share email addresses, and bring them into the business that way. Doing joint promotions and discounts on each others products. We just believed a customer was there to be shared. An online customer was likely to buy from another online store. So think about who are your customers, where do they hang out, how many are there, what does it cost to acquire them, and ultimately, who else is talking to them? If you have a one to many approach in your promotion, you'll have a material impact to your success. Your business will transform and it will grow. It's very hard to go one by one, by one. Try one to many. Who else is talking to your customer? They might be your new best friend.
